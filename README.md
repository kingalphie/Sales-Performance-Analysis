# Sales-Performance-Analysis
I analyzed sales data across segments, countries, and products to understand what drives profit.  The results show how pricing, discounts, and costs influence profitability over time and across markets.

# AIM AND OBJECTIVE OF THIS ANALYSIS

This analysis tends to address the following aims and objectives:   

## SALES PERFORMANCE
Gross Sales and Sales metrics provide a snapshot of total revenue generated from product sales. This is impacted by Units Sold and Sale Price.
Units Sold data, coupled with the Sale Price, reveals the volume of products sold and their market demand.

## PRICING AND DISCOUNTS
Discount Band and Discounts detail the discount strategies applied. Analyzing these factors helps assess their impact on Gross Sales and overall Profit.
Compare the Sale Price with the Manufacturing Price to evaluate pricing strategies and margin adjustments. 

## COST ANALYSIS
Manufacturing Price and COGS (Cost of Goods Sold) provide insight into the production costs and operational expenses associated with the products.
Analyze COGS against Gross Sales to assess cost efficiency and its effect on Profit.

 ## PROFITABILITY
Profit is a critical measure of financial performance, calculated as the difference between Gross Sales and COGS. This helps in evaluating the profitability of different products and sales strategies.

## TEMPORAL TRENDS
Data segmented by Date, Month Number, Month Name, and Year allows for tracking sales trends over time. This helps in identifying seasonal variations, growth patterns, or declines in sales

## GEOGRAPHIC AND PRODUCT INSIGHTS
Country and Product segments reveal regional performance and product-specific trends. This insight is crucial for identifying high-performing markets and products, as well as areas requiring attention.     

# DATA CLEANING AND TRANSFORMATION
Data cleaning and transformation are crucial steps in preparing the sales data for analysis.
## Remove Duplicates:
Identify and remove duplicate records to ensure each entry is unique.
## Handle Missing Values:
Identify missing values in columns such as Discount Band, Units Sold, Manufacturing Price, etc.
Decide on appropriate strategies such as imputation (e.g., mean, median) or removal based on the extent and significance of the missing data.
## Correct Data Types:
Ensure each column has the correct data type (e.g., dates in Date column, numerical values for Units Sold, Sale Price).
Convert data types as needed (e.g., string to datetime).
## Fix Inconsistencies:
Standardize values (e.g., country names or product categories) to ensure consistency across the dataset.
Correct spelling errors or format discrepancies.
## Validate Data Integrity:
Check for logical errors (e.g., Sale Price should not be less than Manufacturing Price).
Verify data ranges and constraints (e.g., Discounts should be within a valid range).

## RECOMMENDATION
The products with low sales should be rebranded and repackaged. Also, they should be sold at a slightly discounted rate, which reduces profit for short period of time, this would help the firm drive more sales and make more profits. The higher the quantity sold at a slightly discounted rate the higher the profit margin accumulated.

Furthermore, advertising those products with low sales would create more awareness and make it possible to push the products to more customers.

The firm should set up a review system for customers to express their satisfaction, complaints and feelings about the thriving products across the countries. This will enable the sales firm to improve the products and create good customer relationship. Thus, this can also be applied in the case of products with low sales.
